   **Step C: Reinforcement Learning Optimization**
   - Use an RL algorithm (commonly **PPO**, Proximal Policy Optimization) to update the main model to **maximize the reward** from the reward model.
   - Usually includes constraints (like a **KL penalty**) to prevent the model from drifting too far from its supervised behavior.

### Why We Use It
- **Better instruction-following:** less wandering, more compliance with the user’s intent.
- **Safer behavior:** reduces toxic, dangerous, or policy-violating outputs.
- **More useful outputs:** clearer, more relevant, better formatted.

### Key Risks / Weak Points (Know the Terrain)
- **Reward hacking:** the model learns to “look good” to the reward model without truly being correct.
- **Bias in feedback:** human preferences can encode cultural or evaluator bias.
- **Over-optimization:** can make responses bland, overly cautious, or evasive.
